Dotan's Revolutionary Kick-Up Rudder
Perfect for Optimist

Attention Optimist Sailors, Parents and Administrators! Houston, we have a problem! Houston, we have a problem! The problem is the standard, non kick-up Optimist rudder. It's been a problem for a long time, decades, in fact, and it's high time to review the situation and try to correct it. Why is it a problem? It's a problem because: It's wrong for children to be expected to wade up to their waist in water in order to install the standard, non kick-up Optimist rudder every time they want to go for a sail, especially when the wind is blowing strongly and the water is cold and choppy. Even at World Championships, children find it difficult to put to sea without the help of trainers or support personnel. It's unfair for children to be made to align the rudder pins with the boat's rocking transom (as children equipped with the anachronistic Optimist rudder need to do). It frustrates many kids. It's unjust to expect children to pull themselves up over the gunwhale into the boat when the boat is floating, when setting sail. It turns many off sailing. It's unjustified for children to be expected to later sit in the boat, soaked wet up to their waists or higher, and then enjoy the sailing as the wind blows on their wet bodies and causes them to be cold (even in the event that they possess wet suits). It's unwarranted that this scenario should repeat itself in reverse when the kids put to shore after their sail. It's ludicrous that this situation be allowed to perpetuate, considering that smart, innovative technology exists which enables kids to push off from the beach, jump into the boat while still dry, and quickly and simply lower their rudders from within the boat. See the Optimists web page for a description of Dotan's child-friendly (and therefore parent-friendly) revolutionary kick-up rudder for Optimists. If you feel the same as we do about this, go to the section 'Start the Revolution NOW!' below.
